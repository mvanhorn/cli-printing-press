# Cutdown Frame Range Selection

The 15-second cutdown is a frame range from the same Hero composition, not a
re-edit. The current chosen range is **frames 270-720** (9.0s through 24.0s
of the hero, total duration 15.0s).

## Why 270-720

This range covers the densest beat of the video:

- **Frame 270 (9.0s)** lands inside the late Problem beat - the agent confusion
  is at peak clutter, just before the hard cut to black at frame 300. Opens
  the cutdown on tension, not setup.
- **Frame 300-330**: hard cut, command types out, music drops. The single
  most important moment of the video.
- **Frame 330-540**: press-stamp #1 transition + phase replay. Shows the
  product working.
- **Frame 540-660**: press-stamp #2 lands. Shows the output (ESPN command).
- **Frame 660-720**: "One command. Every endpoint. Every insight." headline
  begins. The cutdown ends just as the headline lands - leaves the viewer
  on the message, not the next cut.

A cold viewer who sees only this 15s sees: agent thrashing -> hard cut ->
command -> product working -> output -> tagline. That is the entire pitch.

## Alternates considered

- **Frames 300-750**: starts on the cut (no setup tension), gets the full
  Solution beat including the headline hold. Loses the "before" contrast that
  makes the cut feel like a payoff.
- **Frames 450-900**: starts on the press-stamp #1 impact, includes the
  phase replay and ESPN proof cut. Strong middle, but no command-typing
  moment - viewer never sees the hero command. Net loss.

## When to revisit

A/B during U10 ship verification with at least three cold-watch testers per
range. Pick the range whose viewers can recall the URL + command unprompted
60s after watching. Document the chosen range here if it changes.
